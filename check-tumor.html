What is a Tumor?

A brain tumor is a growth of abnormal cells in the brain. Tumors can be benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous) and can affect the brain's normal function depending on their size and location.

Types of Brain Tumors

1. Glioma

Gliomas are tumors that originate in the glial cells of the brain. They are one of the most common types of brain tumors.

2. Meningioma

Meningiomas arise from the meninges, the layers of tissue covering the brain and spinal cord. These tumors are usually benign.

3. Pituitary Tumor

Pituitary tumors develop in the pituitary gland, a small gland at the base of the brain. These tumors are typically non-cancerous but can affect hormone levels.
