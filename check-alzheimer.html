What is Alzheimer's?

Alzheimer's disease is a progressive brain disorder that slowly destroys memory and thinking skills. It leads to cognitive decline, affecting a person's ability to carry out simple tasks. Over time, it severely impacts the brain's normal functioning.

Stages of Alzheimer's

1. No Impairment

In this stage, there are no noticeable symptoms of memory loss or cognitive decline. The individual functions normally, but changes in the brain might begin to occur.

2. Very Mild Impairment

People may start to experience slight memory lapses, such as forgetting familiar words or the location of everyday objects. These symptoms are often attributed to normal aging.

3. Mild Impairment

At this stage, memory issues and cognitive decline become more apparent. Individuals may struggle with recalling recent events or personal information and may have difficulty performing tasks that require concentration.

4. Moderate Impairment

In this stage, individuals exhibit noticeable memory loss and confusion. They may have trouble recognizing familiar people, forget important events, or experience difficulty in performing daily activities without assistance.
